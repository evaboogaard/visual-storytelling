Superheroine

Feminism in DC

  • 1734

    Daniel Boone (1734 - 1820) becomes the father of what we perceive as the modern American superhero, “God was the divine rescuer of the captive. But Boone rescued himself and then his daughter and another girl from captivity. In doing so Boone displaced God as the redeemer. By replacing God, Boone changed American myth.”

    (Coogan, 2018, pg 3)

  • 1941

    Wonder Woman is created by writer William Moulton Marston and artist Harry G. Peter, “Marston drew inspiration from feminist utopian science fiction such as Charlotte Perkins Gilman’s ‘Herland’ (1915) and ‘Her in Ourland’ (1915).

  • 1942

    All-Star Comics 13 (Gardner Fox), “She (wonder woman) is voted the permanent duty of secretary and often stayed behind when the rest of the society rushed off on missions.”

    (Coogan, 2018, pg 7)

  • 1943

    All-Star Comics 17 (Fox, 1943) “Wonder Woman reports to the rest of the JSA that all their meeting notes had been stolen so she returns home to look for them, and the rest of the JSA go off to fight the villainous Brain Wave”

    (Coogan, 2018, pg 7-8)
